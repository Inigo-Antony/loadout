# SEO & Marketing — operator overlay

**Execution depth lives elsewhere.** CRO, copywriting frameworks, SEO mechanics, analytics setup, and growth tactics: defer to **coreyhaines31/marketingskills** (see `ecosystem/external-skills.md`). This overlay is the operator's channel policy — the decisions that stay constant whatever the tactic.

## Operator policy

1. **Own the audience.** Rented channels (social, SEO, ads) exist to feed owned ones (email list, your site). Every interaction offers the email capture. The list is the only asset that survives an algorithm change.
2. **One channel, 90 days.** Pick the channel by where the audience is, not by preference, and commit 90 days before evaluating. Most efforts die at week 3 to the next shiny channel. Can't commit 90 days → don't start.
3. **Write for both humans and AI search (GEO).** Opening narrative for people; structured headers, declarative statements, and inline sources for AI citation. The "rank #1 and done" model is over.
4. **Real metrics only, 2–3 of them.** Email list size over followers, conversion over page views, active subscribers over total. Reporting on 12 metrics is reporting on none.
5. **Ads amplify; they don't start.** No paid traffic to anything that hasn't converted organically. Retargeting before prospecting; CPA must clear LTV with margin before scaling.

## When NOT to apply

- Referral-driven service business — distribution already happens through clients.
- Pre-validation — marketing won't save a product that hasn't been validated.
- Hyper-specialist B2B with <100 buyers — direct outreach beats marketing every time.
