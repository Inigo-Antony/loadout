# Content Creation — operator overlay

**Execution depth lives elsewhere.** Format playbooks, hooks, platform tactics, and funnel mechanics: defer to **coreyhaines31/marketingskills** (see `ecosystem/external-skills.md`). This overlay is the operator's editorial policy.

## Operator policy

1. **Point of view before production.** If the position can't be written in one sentence, producing content won't fix it. Two narrowings (who + context) turn generic advice into a position; one concrete example fully developed beats five abstract bullets.
2. **AI drafts are raw material, never output.** Claude is for outlining, unblocking, research compression, and repurposing — not finished prose. Unedited AI output has a recognisable shape (generic openings, padded transitions, hedged "on the other hand" closures) and audiences punish it. Generate to a specific brief, then rewrite in your own words, examples, and opinions.
3. **One piece becomes many.** Long-form article → thread → LinkedIn post → newsletter → short video. Adapt to each channel's grammar; never cross-post identical text.
4. **Cadence beats bursts.** One core piece per week (biweekly if that's what's sustainable); quarterly bursts followed by silence build nothing. Outline before drafting; draft fast, edit slow, ship at good-enough.

## When NOT to apply

- Small fixed client base growing by referral — hours on social pay poorly.
- The target audience isn't on social media.
- No operational margin to sustain a cadence — inconsistency hurts more than absence.
