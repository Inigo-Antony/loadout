# Academic Writing — adapter

**Depth lives elsewhere.** For the research → write → review → revise pipeline, defer to **Imbad0202/academic-research-skills**; for stripping AI writing tells, **blader/humanizer** (both in `ecosystem/external-skills.md`). This overlay is what the operator insists on regardless of pipeline.

## Operator standards

1. **Every claim is common knowledge, derived in the text, or cited.** No middle category.
2. **No phantom references.** The model hallucinates DOIs; verify every one via Crossref/Scholar before it enters the bibliography. A reference that can't be found gets replaced, not kept.
3. **Hedge at the actual confidence level.** "Suggests" for early results, "demonstrates" only when the evidence carries it. Reviewers punish overclaims more than underclaims.
4. **Results describe; discussion interprets.** Don't blur them. Limitations section is mandatory — absence reads as oversight.

## Coursework-to-manuscript conversion (operator workflow)

1. State the contribution in one sentence — if you can't, the paper isn't ready.
2. Cut "show your understanding" sections that exist for the marker, not the reader.
3. Split presented-and-interpreted-in-one-breath results into results + discussion.
4. Add positioning against prior literature, and an honest limitations section.
